- ✅ **Complaint tracking** to close the loop

**Notice the difference?** Most of what expensive apps offer are features designed for large corporate townships (500+ flats), not your 50-flat community.

## Why Expensive Doesn't Mean Better

### Over-Engineered Solutions

**Problem:** Expensive platforms are built for enterprise needs.

**Example:** A ₹4,000/month platform has a full HR module for managing 20+ security guards, housekeeping staff, and maintenance workers. Your society has 2 guards and 1 cleaner.

**Reality:** You're paying for features you'll never use.

### Complex Implementation

**Problem:** Enterprise software requires extensive training and adoption time.

**Example:** One secretary told us their expensive platform took 4 months to fully implement, with multiple training sessions, committee meetings, and resident workshops.

**Reality:** Your volunteer committee members don't have time to become software experts.

### Technology Barriers

**Problem:** Dedicated apps require everyone to download and learn new software.

**Example:** In a 100-flat society that adopted an expensive app, only 45% of residents ever downloaded it. The secretary still had to send updates via WhatsApp anyway.

**Reality:** Residents won't adopt technology that adds friction to their daily lives.
